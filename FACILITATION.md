# Facilitation

How pod blocks run, what facilitators do, and what that means for everyone building pod materials. Five pods of ten participants, same trainer and peer group across both days.

## What a pod facilitator does

The facilitator runs the room, not the content. The materials carry the content. During a pod block:

- Walk the pod through the exercise steps in order and keep everyone moving.
- Keep the time. Announce the timebox at the start of each stage, signal halfway, and start the wrap-up early enough that nobody gets cut off mid-thought.
- Hold a short reflection after each stage: what worked, what surprised you, what changes in your factory. Five minutes of honest conversation beats finishing every step.
- Make exchange happen. Pair people when someone is stuck, pull quiet participants in, ask those with a working setup to show it to the others.

## What this means for pod materials

Build exercises so a facilitator can run them straight from the README. Every exercise folder (`modules/<module>/exercises/<exercise>/`) ships:

- a one-sentence goal: what exists at the end
- numbered steps, each with its own timebox
- an expected outcome the facilitator can check at a glance
- two or three reflection prompts for the conversation after the stage
- starter files where useful, and a `solution/` folder for reference

Write for mixed maturity: give fast pairs a stretch task and stuck pairs a fallback path.

## Show & tell commitment

Each pod sends a person or group to the Cross-pod show & tell on Day 2 (12:00-13:00) to present their factory: the use case, what worked, what broke. Facilitators surface candidates during the Day 1 pod blocks and confirm their pod's presenter by the end of Day 1, so nobody scrambles on Day 2.
